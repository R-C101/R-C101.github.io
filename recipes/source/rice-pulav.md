# Rice Pulav (High-Protein)

One-pot pulav with a fresh-ground coconut-garlic-chilli masala. Bulked with soya chunks so it actually hits protein.

- **Original Chef:** Yashasree Sawarkar
- **Serves:** 2
- **Prep:** 15 min
- **Cook:** 25 min
- **Total:** ~40 min

## Ingredients

### The protein
- **100 g dry soya chunks** (50 g per serving — scale this with servings, that's the whole point)
- Boiling water, to soak

### The grind (masala paste)
- 3 tbsp dry coconut (desiccated/kopra), ~20 g
- 1 inch ginger (adrak)
- 6 cloves garlic (lasun)
- 2 green chillies
- 1 inch cinnamon stick (dalchini)
- Splash of water to help it blend

### The base
- 1 cup basmati rice (~185 g dry), rinsed until the water runs clear
- 2 tbsp oil
- 2 cups mixed vegetables (~250 g) — potato, cauliflower, green beans, peas, whatever you like
- 2 cups hot water (2:1 water to rice)

### Ground spices
- 1/2 tsp turmeric (haldi)
- 1 tsp red chilli powder
- 1 tsp coriander powder (dhaniya powder)
- 1/2 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp salt, to taste

### To finish
- 3 tbsp fresh coriander (hara dhaniya), chopped

---

## Steps

1. **Prep the soya chunks.** Soak **100 g dry soya chunks** in boiling water for 10 minutes until soft. Drain, then **squeeze them dry hard** — get all the water out or they'll taste bland and turn the pulav watery. Squeeze, refill with fresh water, squeeze again. Set aside.

2. **Rinse the rice.** Rinse **1 cup basmati (~185 g)** until the water runs clear. Soak for 15 minutes, then drain. (Soaking is optional but gives longer, fluffier grains.)

3. **Chop the veg.** Cut **2 cups (~250 g) mixed vegetables** — potato, cauliflower, beans, peas — into bite-sized pieces.

4. **Grind the masala.** Blend **3 tbsp dry coconut + 1 inch ginger + 6 cloves garlic + 2 green chillies + 1 inch cinnamon** with a splash of water into a coarse paste.

5. **Bhuno the masala.** Heat **2 tbsp oil** in a heavy pot over medium heat. Add the ground paste and fry 3–4 minutes, stirring, until the raw smell is gone and the oil starts to separate at the edges.

6. **Toast the soya.** Add the squeezed-dry **soya chunks** and fry 3–4 minutes until they pick up colour and start catching on the pot. This step is what makes them taste good instead of spongy — don't skip it.

7. **Toast the rice.** Add the drained **rice** and fry gently 2 minutes, stirring, until each grain is coated in oil and slightly translucent. Be gentle — you don't want broken grains.

8. **Add the ground spices.** Add **1/2 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp red chilli powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, 1/2 tsp garam masala, 1 tsp salt**. Stir for 1 minute to bloom the spices.

9. **Add the vegetables.** Tip in the **2 cups chopped vegetables** and stir for 2 minutes to coat.

10. **Add hot water and cook.** Pour in **2 cups hot water** (hot, not cold — cold water shocks the rice and makes it cook unevenly). Stir once, bring to a boil, then cover and drop to the lowest heat. Cook **12–15 minutes** until the water is absorbed and the rice is tender.

11. **Rest.** Turn off the heat and leave it covered for **5 minutes**. Don't lift the lid. Then fluff with a fork.

12. **Finish.** Scatter **3 tbsp chopped fresh coriander** over the top.
